This web map is based on a scanned copy of Libor Bayer’s 1879 plan of Stara Zagora, made publicly available by the Regional History Museum of Stara Zagora. The original scan is available here: https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/54/Stara_Zagora,_1879_rebuild_plan.jpg.
The original map is damaged in several areas. As a result, the georeferencing process has introduced slight inaccuracies, and the digital image is somewhat distorted in places, particularly in the southeastern quarter of the map.
Some of the information displayed in the web map does not appear on the original plan itself. The names of the churches and mosques, where identifiable, have been supplied from external sources.
The classification of roads and streets is, for the most part, the interpretation of the author of the web map. On the original plan, Bayer labeled only those road segments leading to Kazanlak, Nova Zagora, Seymen (present-day Simeonovgrad), and Chirpan.
Some features not explicitly identified on the original plan have been interpreted as watermills. This identification is based on their location along the Bedechka River and on comparison with features shown on the so-called Three-Verst Map, produced by the Russian Military Topographic Depot during the Russo-Turkish War of 1877–1878.
The location of the Chifte Hamam and the line of the Roman town walls are not indicated on Bayer’s plan and have been added by the author of the web map, as both are important elements of the historical urban structure and are relevant to the study of the origins, organization, and development of the Ottoman town.
